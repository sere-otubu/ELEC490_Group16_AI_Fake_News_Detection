SEARCH_TOPIC: Burnout syndrome
TITLE: Coping Strategies of Healthcare Professionals with Burnout Syndrome: A Systematic Review.
PMID: 35208650
SOURCE_URL: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/35208650/
TYPE: PubMed Medical Abstract

ABSTRACT:
<i>Background and Objectives:</i> To evaluate the efficacy of coping strategies used to reduce burnout syndrome in healthcare workers teams. <i>Materials and Methods:</i> We used PubMed and Web of Science, including scientific articles and other studies for additional citations. Only 7 of 906 publications have the appropriate inclusion criteria and were selected. A PRISMA 2020 flow diagram was used. <i>Results:</i> The most common coping strategies that the literature studies showed were efficient, in particular social and emotional support, physical activity, physical self-care, emotional and physical distancing from work. Coping mechanisms associated with less burnout were also physical well-being, clinical variety, setting boundaries, transcendental, passion for one's work, realistic expectations, remembering patients and organizational activities. Furthermore, it was helpful to listen to the team's needs and preferences about some types of training. <i>Conclusion:</i> We suppose that the appropriate coping strategies employed in the team could be useful also in the prevention of psychological suffering, especially in contexts where working conditions are stressful. Studies about coping strategies to face burnout syndrome in healthcare workers should be increased.
