
    SOURCE: PubMed QA (ID: 23598835)
    QUESTION: Is severe pain immediately after spinal augmentation a predictor of long-term outcomes?
    CONTEXT: Severe, immediate postprocedural pain and the need for analgesics after vertebroplasty can be a discouraging experience for patients and caregivers. The goal of this study was to investigate whether the presence of severe pain immediately after vertebroplasty predicts short- and long-term pain relief.
    ANSWER: Patients with severe pain immediately after vertebroplasty have similar long-term outcomes compared with patients without severe pain.
