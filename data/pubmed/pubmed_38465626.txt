SEARCH_TOPIC: Healthcare accessibility
TITLE: How healthcare systems are experienced by autistic adults in the United Kingdom: A meta-ethnography.
PMID: 38465626
SOURCE_URL: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38465626/
TYPE: PubMed Medical Abstract

ABSTRACT:
Autistic adults are more likely to experience mental and physical health difficulties, and yet can find it difficult to get the support that they need. A meta-ethnographic approach was used to review the existing research on autistic adults' experiences of accessing healthcare. Four databases were searched for qualitative and mixed-method studies which looked at the experiences of autistic adults who did not also have a learning disability when using healthcare services in the United Kingdom. Fifteen papers met the criteria to be included, and seven steps were used to analyse the information and develop new themes. Three main themes were identified: <i>Professionals' lack of knowledge can be damaging</i>, <i>Need to reduce processing demands</i> and <i>Adaptation to improve engagement.</i> This review highlights how damaging misdiagnosis, inappropriate treatment, overwhelming environments and systems that are difficult to access can have on the well-being of autistic adults. Limited knowledge and understanding about autism knowledge among healthcare professionals along with autistic adult's own communication and sensory differences indicate that there is a need for improved training developed with autistic adults and adaptations.
