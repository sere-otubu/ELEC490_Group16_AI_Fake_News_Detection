
    SOURCE: PubMed QA (ID: 17259061)
    QUESTION: Cigarettes and cinema: does parental restriction of R-rated movie viewing reduce adolescent smoking susceptibility?
    CONTEXT: To examine the relationship between exposure to pro-smoking messages in media and susceptibility to smoking adoption among middle school students. The hypothesis that parental restriction of R-rated movie viewing is associated with lower adolescent smoking susceptibility was tested.
    ANSWER: Parental restriction of R-rated movie viewing is associated with both lower adolescent smoking susceptibility and lower smoking rates.
