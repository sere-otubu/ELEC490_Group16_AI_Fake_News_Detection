
    SOURCE: PubMed QA (ID: 12094116)
    QUESTION: Is muscle power related to running speed with changes of direction?
    CONTEXT: The purpose of this study was to identify the relationships between leg muscle power and sprinting speed with changes of direction.
    ANSWER: The relationships between leg muscle power and change-of-direction speed were not consistent. Reactive strength as measured by the drop jump appears to have some importance for lateral change-of-direction speed, possibly because of similar push-off actions. It was concluded that reactive strength of the leg extensor muscles has some importance in change-of-direction performance but the other technical and perceptual factors than influence agility performance should also be considered.
