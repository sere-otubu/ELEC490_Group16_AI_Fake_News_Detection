
    SOURCE: PubMed QA (ID: 22876568)
    QUESTION: Is vitamin D deficiency a feature of pediatric celiac disease?
    CONTEXT: Celiac disease (CD) is an autoimmune enteropathy characterized by villus atrophy and malabsorption of essential nutrients. Vitamin D deficiency has been described in autoimmune diseases, but its status in prepubertal children with CD has not been adequately studied.
    ANSWER: Our data showed no difference in 25(OH) D levels between normal children and those with CD when adjusted for body mass index.
