
    SOURCE: PubMed QA (ID: 14652839)
    QUESTION: Does the sequence of clamp application during open abdominal aortic aneurysm surgery influence distal embolisation?
    CONTEXT: Embolisation of atherosclerotic debris during abdominal aortic aneurysm (AAA) repair is responsible for significant peri-operative morbidity. Reports have suggested that preferential clamping of the distal vessel(s) before the proximal aorta may decrease the number of emboli passing distally and hence reduce complications.
    ANSWER: There appears to be no difference in the numbers of emboli detected when the first vascular clamp is applied to the proximal aorta or iliacs.
