
    SOURCE: PubMed QA (ID: 24577079)
    QUESTION: Does strategy training reduce age-related deficits in working memory?
    CONTEXT: Older adults typically perform worse on measures of working memory (WM) than do young adults; however, age-related differences in WM performance might be reduced if older adults use effective encoding strategies.
    ANSWER: Strategy training can boost WM performance, and its benefits appear to arise from strategy-specific effects and not from domain-general gains in cognitive ability.
