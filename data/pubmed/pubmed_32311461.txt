SEARCH_TOPIC: DNA structure
TITLE: A dynamic view of DNA structure within the nucleosome: Biological implications.
PMID: 32311461
SOURCE_URL: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/32311461/
TYPE: PubMed Medical Abstract

ABSTRACT:
Most of eukaryotic cellular DNA is packed in nucleosome core particles (NCPs), in which the DNA (DNA<sub>NCP</sub>) is wrapped around histones. The influence of this organization on the intrinsic local dynamics of DNA is largely unknown, in particular because capturing such information from experiments remains notoriously challenging. Given the importance of dynamical properties in DNA functions, we addressed this issue using CHARMM36 MD simulations of a nucleosome containing the NCP positioning 601 sequence and four related free dodecamers. Comparison between DNA<sub>NCP</sub> and free DNA reveals a limited impact of the dense DNA-histone interface on correlated motions of dinucleotide constituents and on fluctuations of inter base pair parameters. A characteristic feature intimately associated with the DNA<sub>NCP</sub> super-helical path is a set of structural periodicities that includes a marked alternation of regions enriched in backbone BI and BII conformers. This observation led to uncover a convincing correspondence between the sequence effect on BI/BII propensities in both DNA<sub>NCP</sub> and free DNA, strengthening the idea that the histone preference for particular DNA sequences relies on those intrinsic structural properties. These results offer for the first time a detailed view of the DNA dynamical behavior within NCP. They show in particular that the DNA<sub>NCP</sub> dynamics is substantial enough to preserve the ability to structurally adjust to external proteins, for instance remodelers. Also, fresh structural arguments highlight the relevance of relationships between DNA sequence and structural properties for NCP formation. Overall, our work offers a more rational framework to approach the functional, biological roles of NCP.
