SEARCH_TOPIC: Blood pressure regulation
TITLE: Regulation of Blood Pressure by Targeting Ca<sub>V</sub>1.2-Galectin-1 Protein Interaction.
PMID: 29650545
SOURCE_URL: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/29650545/
TYPE: PubMed Medical Abstract

ABSTRACT:
L-type Ca<sub>V</sub>1.2 channels play crucial roles in the regulation of blood pressure. Galectin-1 (Gal-1) has been reported to bind to the I-II loop of Ca<sub>V</sub>1.2 channels to reduce their current density. However, the mechanistic understanding for the downregulation of Ca<sub>V</sub>1.2 channels by Gal-1 and whether Gal-1 plays a direct role in blood pressure regulation remain unclear. In vitro experiments involving coimmunoprecipitation, Western blot, patch-clamp recordings, immunohistochemistry, and pressure myography were used to evaluate the molecular mechanisms by which Gal-1 downregulates Ca<sub>V</sub>1.2 channel in transfected, human embryonic kidney 293 cells, smooth muscle cells, arteries from Lgasl1<sup>-/-</sup> mice, rat, and human patients. In vivo experiments involving the delivery of Tat-e9c peptide and AAV5-Gal-1 into rats were performed to investigate the effect of targeting Ca<sub>V</sub>1.2-Gal-1 interaction on blood pressure monitored by tail-cuff or telemetry methods. Our study reveals that Gal-1 is a key regulator for proteasomal degradation of Ca<sub>V</sub>1.2 channels. Gal-1 competed allosterically with the Ca<sub>V</sub>β subunit for binding to the I-II loop of the Ca<sub>V</sub>1.2 channel. This competitive disruption of Ca<sub>V</sub>β binding led to Ca<sub>V</sub>1.2 degradation by exposing the channels to polyubiquitination. It is notable that we demonstrated that the inverse relationship of reduced Gal-1 and increased Ca<sub>V</sub>1.2 protein levels in arteries was associated with hypertension in hypertensive rats and patients, and Gal-1 deficiency induces higher blood pressure in mice because of the upregulated Ca<sub>V</sub>1.2 protein level in arteries. To directly regulate blood pressure by targeting the Ca<sub>V</sub>1.2-Gal-1 interaction, we administered Tat-e9c, a peptide that competed for binding of Gal-1 by a miniosmotic pump, and this specific disruption of Ca<sub>V</sub>1.2-Gal-1 coupling increased smooth muscle Ca<sub>V</sub>1.2 currents, induced larger arterial contraction, and caused hypertension in rats. In contrasting experiments, overexpression of Gal-1 in smooth muscle by a single bolus of AAV5-Gal-1 significantly reduced blood pressure in spontaneously hypertensive rats. We have defined molecularly that Gal-1 promotes Ca<sub>V</sub>1.2 degradation by replacing Ca<sub>V</sub>β and thereby exposing specific lysines for polyubiquitination and by masking I-II loop endoplasmic reticulum export signals. This mechanistic understanding provided the basis for targeting Ca<sub>V</sub>1.2-Gal-1 interaction to demonstrate clearly the modulatory role that Gal-1 plays in regulating blood pressure, and offering a potential approach for therapeutic management of hypertension.
