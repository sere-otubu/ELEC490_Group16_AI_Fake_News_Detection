
    SOURCE: PubMed QA (ID: 21904069)
    QUESTION: Spleen-preserving distal pancreatectomy with resection of the splenic vessels. Should one rely on the short gastric arteries?
    CONTEXT: Knowing the collaterals is essential for a spleen-preserving distal pancreatectomy with resection of the splenic vessels.
    ANSWER: Experimental, intra- and postoperative instrumental investigations did not show the short gastric arteries to be engaged in the blood supply to the spleen after a spleen-preserving distal pancreatectomy with resection of the splenic vessels. In all cases, the left gastroepiploic artery was the main collateral vessel.
