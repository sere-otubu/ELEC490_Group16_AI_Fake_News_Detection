
    SOURCE: PubMed QA (ID: 20736887)
    QUESTION: Is decompressive surgery effective for spinal cord sarcoidosis accompanied with compressive cervical myelopathy?
    CONTEXT: A retrospective multicenter study of series of 12 patients with spinal cord sarcoidosis who underwent surgery.
    ANSWER: The effect of decompression for spinal cord sarcoidosis with compressive myelopathy was not evident. Early diagnosis for sarcoidosis from other organ and steroid therapy should be needed.
