SEARCH_TOPIC: E. coli infection
TITLE: <i>Tm</i>Spz-like Plays a Fundamental Role in Response to <i>E. coli</i> but Not <i>S. aureus</i> or <i>C. albican</i> Infection in <i>Tenebrio molitor</i> via Regulation of Antimicrobial Peptide Production.
PMID: 34639230
SOURCE_URL: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/34639230/
TYPE: PubMed Medical Abstract

ABSTRACT:
The cystine knot protein Spätzle is a Toll receptor ligand that modulates the intracellular signaling cascade involved in the nuclear factor kappa B (NF-κB)-mediated regulation of antimicrobial peptide (AMP)-encoding genes. Spätzle-mediated activation of the Toll pathway is critical for the innate immune responses of insects against Gram-positive bacteria and fungi. In this study, the open reading frame (ORF) sequence of <i>Spätzle-like</i> from <i>T. molitor</i> (<i>TmSpz-like</i>) identified from the RNA sequencing dataset was cloned and sequenced. The 885-bp <i>TmSpz-like</i> ORF encoded a polypeptide of 294 amino acid residues. <i>Tm</i>Spz-like comprised a cystine knot domain with six conserved cysteine residues that formed three disulfide bonds. Additionally, <i>Tm</i>Spz-like exhibited the highest amino acid sequence similarity with <i>T. castaneum</i> Spätzle (<i>Tc</i>Spz). In the phylogenetic tree, <i>Tm</i>Spz-like and <i>Tc</i>Spz were located within a single cluster. The expression of <i>TmSpz-like</i> was upregulated in the Malpighian tubules and gut tissues of <i>T. molitor</i>. Additionally, the expression of <i>TmSpz-like</i> in the whole body and gut of the larvae was upregulated at 24 h post-<i>E. coli</i> infection. The results of RNA interference experiments revealed that <i>TmSpz-like</i> is critical for the viability of <i>E. coli</i>-infected <i>T. molitor</i> larvae. Eleven AMP-encoding genes were downregulated in the <i>E. coli</i>-infected <i>TmSpz-like</i> knockdown larvae, which suggested that <i>TmSpz-like</i> positively regulated these genes. Additionally, the NF-κB-encoding genes (<i>TmDorX1</i>, <i>TmDorX2</i>, and <i>TmRelish</i>) were downregulated in the <i>E. coli</i>-infected <i>TmSpz-like</i> knockdown larvae. Thus, <i>TmSpz-like</i> plays a critical role in the regulation of AMP production in <i>T. molitor</i> in response to <i>E. coli</i> infection.
