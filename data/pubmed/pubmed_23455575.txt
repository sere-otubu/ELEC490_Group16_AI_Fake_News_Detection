    QUESTION: Globulomaxillary cysts--do they really exist?
    CONTEXT: The so-called "globulomaxillary cyst", described as a fissural cyst, caused by entrapped epithelium between the nasal and maxillary process, is no longer considered for its own entity. Nevertheless, cystic lesions, which correspond to the previous image of globulomaxillary cysts, do still occur in daily practice. This raises the question to which entities pathological processes in this particular region actually belong to.
    ANSWER: According to the results of our study and the data from the international literature, the entity globulomaxillary cyst is no longer justified.
