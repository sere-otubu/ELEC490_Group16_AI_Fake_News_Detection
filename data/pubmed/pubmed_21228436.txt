
    SOURCE: PubMed QA (ID: 21228436)
    QUESTION: Can teaching medical students to investigate medication errors change their attitudes towards patient safety?
    CONTEXT: The purpose of this study was to evaluate the impact of a patient-safety curriculum administered during a paediatric clerkship on medical students' attitudes towards patient safety.
    ANSWER: An active learning curriculum integrated into a clinical clerkship can change learners' attitudes towards patient safety. Students found the curriculum relevant and recommended its continuation.
