
    SOURCE: PubMed QA (ID: 7547656)
    QUESTION: Does continuous intravenous infusion of low-concentration epinephrine impair uterine blood flow in pregnant ewes?
    CONTEXT: Bolus intravenous injection of epinephrine can decrease uterine blood flow. This study examined the effects of intravenous infusion of epinephrine on uterine blood flow in the gravid ewe.
    ANSWER: In gravid ewes, intravenous infusion of<or = 1.67 micrograms/min epinephrine altered neither maternal hemodynamics nor uterine blood flow. To the extent that sheep data can be extrapolated to humans, these results suggest that continuous intravenous infusion of epinephrine in local anesthetic solutions is safe if the epidural catheter should enter a blood vessel during the infusion.
