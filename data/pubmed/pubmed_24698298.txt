
    SOURCE: PubMed QA (ID: 24698298)
    QUESTION: MR arthrography of the shoulder: do we need local anesthesia?
    CONTEXT: To assess pain intensity with and without subcutaneous local anesthesia prior to intraarticular administration of contrast medium for magnetic resonance arthrography (MRa) of the shoulder.
    ANSWER: Local anesthesia is not required to lower a patient's pain intensity when applying intra-articular contrast media for MR arthrography of the shoulder. This could result in reduced costs and a reduced risk of adverse reactions, without an impact on patient comfort.
