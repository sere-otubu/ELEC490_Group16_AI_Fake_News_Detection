Title: An Unusual Suspect: Hyperprolactinemia From Nicotine Pouches.

Abstract: Elevated prolactin levels are associated with galactorrhea, menstrual irregularities, or infertility. While nicotine is known to affect prolactin secretion via dopaminergic pathways, noncombustible nicotine products such as pouches are rarely considered as contributing factors. The objective of this report is to describe a patient with hyperprolactinemia associated with nicotine pouch use, highlighting a potentially overlooked etiology.
