Title: Change of oral microbiome diversity by smoking across different age groups.

Abstract: The oral microbiome, a complex ecosystem linked to both oral and systemic diseases, undergoes compositional and functional changes with aging. Tobacco exposure is a known disruptor of microbial homeostasis, yet its effect on microbial diversity remains inconsistent. Whether agingmodifies the relationship between smoking and the oral microbiome remains unclear. This study aimed to evaluate (1) the association between serum cotinine and oral microbial diversity, (2) whether this association varies by age, and (3) taxonomic shifts that may explain smoking-related dysbiosis.
