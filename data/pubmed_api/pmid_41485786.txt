Title: Quercetin attenuates nicotine-induced ovarian and uterine dysfunction in rats.

Abstract: Nicotine, a principal constituent of tobacco smoke, disrupts female reproductive physiology. Quercetin, a flavonoid with both phytoestrogenic and antioxidant properties, may counteract this toxicity. This study aimed to evaluate the histological, biochemical, and molecular effects of quercetin co-administration with nicotine on ovarian and uterine tissues in rats.
