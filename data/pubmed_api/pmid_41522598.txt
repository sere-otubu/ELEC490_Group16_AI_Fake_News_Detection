Title: Behavioral, Psychological, and Environmental Determinants of Nicotine Dependence Among Medical Students: A Multifactorial Approach.

Abstract: Nicotine dependence is a significant public health issue, influenced by behavioral, psychological, and environmental factors. Medical students, who face unique stressors, are particularly susceptible to tobacco use, potentially affecting their future healthcare roles. This study aims to identify interrelationships for targeted interventions.
