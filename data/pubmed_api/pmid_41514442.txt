Title: Bacterial extracellular vesicles in colorectal cancer: mechanisms, biomarker potential, and therapeutic engineering.

Abstract: Colorectal cancer (CRC) is a leading cause of cancer-related deaths. The human colon, which hosts a vast and complex microbial ecosystem, plays a crucial role in health and disease, with approximately 20% of human cancers linked to microbial dysbiosis. Bacterial extracellular vesicles (BEVs) serve as critical mediators of host-microbe communication, influencing both physiological and pathological processes.
