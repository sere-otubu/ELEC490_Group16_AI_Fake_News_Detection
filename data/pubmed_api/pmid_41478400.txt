Title: Cx43 phosphorylation at Ser368 facilitates PASMC dedifferentiation in nicotine-induced pulmonary arterial remodeling.

Abstract: Pulmonary arterial hypertension is a severe disease characterized by pulmonary vascular remodeling, which is closely associated with the phenotypic switching of pulmonary artery smooth muscle cells (PASMCs). Connexin 43 (Cx43) phosphorylation is a key regulator of intercellular communication. However, the specific mechanism underlying nicotine-induced dedifferentiation of PASMCs remains unclear.
