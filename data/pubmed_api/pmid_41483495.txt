Title: Inequities in blunt use across multiple socio-demographic intersections among US adults.

Abstract: Blunts (cigars with tobacco replaced or mixed with cannabis) incur health risks from exposure to tobacco and cannabis and are disproportionately used by young adults, people identifying as Black/African American (B/AA), people with lower socioeconomic status (SES), and people with mental health issues. This study described patterns of blunt use among US adults with multiple identities or circumstances increasing risk for blunt use.
