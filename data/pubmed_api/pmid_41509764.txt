Title: Platelet-Rich Plasma Injections are Associated with Higher Resource Utilization and Surgical Escalation Compared with Corticosteroids in Lateral Elbow Tendinopathy.

Abstract: Epicondylitis (lateral and medial) is a common tendinopathy that impairs function and quality of life. Corticosteroid injections (CSI) provide rapid but often short-lived symptom relief, while platelet-rich plasma (PRP) is used as a biologic alternative aimed at tendon regeneration. Comparative effectiveness between PRP and CSI remains uncertain in real-world settings.
